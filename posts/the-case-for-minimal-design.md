# The Case for Minimal Design

Minimalism in design isn't about making things look bare — it's about removing everything that doesn't serve a purpose.

## Less is Harder

Designing minimally is more difficult than adding features. Every element that remains must earn its place. This constraint forces better decisions.

> "Perfection is achieved not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

## In Practice

On the web, minimal design means:

- Fast load times
- Readable typography
- Generous whitespace
- One clear action per screen

When in doubt, remove it.
